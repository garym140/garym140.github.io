Craigslist 2.0

The Problem

Currently, craiglist's website is a disaster. Yet, people still use it because it's resourceful. Here's a list of things we found particulary terrible through both user research and personal experience:

1. Home page has too many options.
2. Is there a logo?
3. What are the abbreviated letters at the top?
4. Overwhelming amount of information.
5. Why is there a calendar.
6. What does 'gig' mean?
7. Some content is left aligned while others are center alligned.

Solutions

It was clear that a sense of information heirarchy needed to be established in this website.

Aesthetically, there needs to be a clear brand statement with a logo, color pallete, and set typography.

Navigation, A clear form of navigation through layout and reduced content also needs to be created.

Paper Prototype

Testing + Feedback

After testing our paper protoype some of the feedback included:

1. Add more options to the nav bar.
2. Reduce the sketchy element of Craigslist. Make it feel safer.
3. What categories do you use the most? Many said: selling, buying, and housing.
4. Would like to see the most recent items in a more organized manner.

Style-Guide

We used some of the feedback to create our user flow diagram.

Then I started to develop the style-guide and branding.
